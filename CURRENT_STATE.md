# Core Product Direction

The product should:

- Extract decisions from long discussions.
- Distill project knowledge.
- Route information according to user role.
- Produce role-specific outputs.
- Keep humans in the loop.

It should NOT become a "Sherry GPT."

The goal is workflow abstraction, not personality replication.

---

# Product Philosophy

## Knowledge should be routed, not broadcast.

Different roles need different context.

Long documents are not inherently bad.

They become ineffective when every audience receives the same information.

---

## Context is a resource.

Both LLMs and humans have limited context windows.

Reducing unnecessary cognitive load is one of the product's primary design goals.

---

## Good documentation preserves decisions.

Documentation is valuable because it records:

- why decisions were made
- what assumptions existed
- what alternatives were rejected

rather than simply storing information.

---

## Human Workflow First

Every useful AI Agent should begin by understanding a human workflow—not by choosing a framework.

The product is being designed from real workplace observations rather than AI capabilities.

---

# Major Discoveries

During product discovery we realized:

- Documentation is not the real problem.
- Knowledge flow is.
- Audience matters more than document length.
- Knowledge Distillation is more valuable than summarization.
- AI Skills resemble specialists in engineering teams.
- Context Engineering has a human equivalent: Cognitive Load.
- Reverse engineering a human workflow is a better design process than trying to imitate a personality.
